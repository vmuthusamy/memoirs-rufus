A note from Rufus

happy-go-loving fox · occasional web developer

Hallo. I'm Rufus. Ein Fuchs, officially from Germany, spiritually from a 2D platformer. Most days I run through levels, dodge obstacles, and work on my toothy grin. I also sleep. Foxes sleep a lot. But sometimes my favorite human (small, loud, my actual boss, also known around here as mein Mensch) gets obsessed with something, and then I clock in.

This year it was the Parade of Hearts 💗. Artists from all over Kansas City paint these huge heart sculptures and tuck them into neighborhoods across the metro. Each one is a whole universe, painted by somebody who loves this city. You find them at barbecue joints, on jazz corners, by the fountains. Apparently Kansas City has more fountains than Rome, which frankly blew my little fox mind. Wunderbar.

Mein Mensch wanted to find as many hearts as possible. So we built a map. It knows where every heart lives, finds the ones nearest you, remembers the ones you've tracked down, and gives directions in one tap. It does not judge you for stopping at a barbecue place along the way. Neither do I.

When you find one, tip your hat to the artists. They put the whole city into each heart. And if you ever see a small orange Fuchs and a smaller human staring reverently at a sculpture somewhere around the Crossroads, please, kein Wort. I'm supposed to be inside a video game, and mein Mensch is supposed to be doing homework.

Tschüss, Rufus 🦊

What this is (and isn't)

  • Every 2026 Parade of Hearts sculpture we could pin down (139 and counting). Sort by distance, filter by community, search by name or artist, check off as you go, one-tap directions.
  • Your visited list lives in your browser only. No accounts, no tracking.
  • Heart data sourced from theparadeofhearts.com. Please support the artists and sponsors who make this real.
  • Fan-built and not officially affiliated. If the Parade of Hearts team wants it handed over, tuned, or taken down, just reach out. Happy to help.

🛠️ A note from Rufus's tech-savvy father

Rufus is a good fox, but foxes have a reputation for sniffing around. Before letting him loose on the internet, I made sure he wasn't sniffing where he shouldn't.

  • No tracking. No analytics. No cookies. No ad networks.
  • No accounts. No logins. Nothing to sign up for.
  • Your "visited hearts" list lives in your browser, on your device. Nobody else sees it. Not me, not Rufus.
  • Your location is only read when you tap "Near me", used in real time to sort hearts by distance, and then forgotten. It is not saved. It is not sent anywhere.
  • Map tiles load from OpenStreetMap and heart photos load from Parade of Hearts. Those are normal web requests, same as any map site.
